### 1. Instrumental Approaches in Neuropharmacological Screening: A Review of Insights from Electroconvulsiometer and Actophotometer Studies
- The Electroconvulsiometer and Actophotometer are essential tools in neuropharmacology for evaluating CNS-active drugs. The Electroconvulsiometer models maximal electroshock seizures to assess anticonvulsant properties, while the Actophotometer measures locomotor activity to determine CNS stimulant or depressant effects. Together, they enable objective preclinical screening of neurological drug actions.

### 2. Sitophilus oryzae: A comprehensive review of its biology, infestation dynamics and management strategies**
The rice weevil (*Sitophilus oryzae*) is a major global stored-grain pest causing significant post-harvest losses and reduced grain quality. While chemical pesticides provide control, concerns about resistance and environmental impact highlight the need for safer alternatives. This review summarizes its biology and feeding behavior while emphasizing sustainable IPM approaches, including botanicals, biocontrol, physical methods, and emerging molecular tools.

-  Spinal muscular atrophy (SMA) is a severe autosomal recessive neuromuscular disorder caused by mutations in the SMN1 gene, with disease severity influenced by SMN2 copy number. It leads to progressive muscle weakness, atrophy, and respiratory issues, ranging from severe infantile to milder later-onset forms. Recent molecular therapies‚Äîincluding antisense oligonucleotides, gene therapy, and splicing modifiers‚Äîhave transformed SMA into a treatable condition. Supportive care, especially nutritional management, remains vital to address swallowing difficulties, altered metabolism, and feeding challenges, improving health outcomes and quality of life. A multidisciplinary approach is crucial for optimal patient care.
